## Notes

The takeaways are that there are 5 factors that undermine a team, even when the individual members are strong, and they are interconnected. Exploring them with focused sessions and clear communication is the leader's job, in this example the CEO.

- ABSENCE OF TRUST
Not trusting others to do their job, and lack of self-confidence, prevents cooperation and weakens the foundation. ‚úÖ First step is to open up, talk about yourself and your personal challenges, starting with the boss. Create a sense of transparency
- FEAR OF CONFLICT
Dull meetings where no one challenges others' ideas, so no problems get solved in the name of keeping harmony ‚úÖ Understand that conflict, and exchanging views, is productive. With respect for others, of course. The leader should keep it reasonable
- LACK OF COMMITMENT
Clear goals, deadlines, and assessments are needed so that people do what they promise ‚úÖ Cascading message, everyone should relay things to their teams right away, and deadlines in black and white. Be patient with mistakes
- AVOIDANCE OF ACCOUNTABILITY
To avoid crises and discomfort, no one takes responsibility and everything gets pushed onto others. No one takes ownership ‚úÖ Public goals with a clear, specific owner. Reward results
- INATTENTION TO RESULTS (EGO)
A final consequence is that everyone pulls in their own direction and there are endless disputes that lead nowhere. The overall direction is falling apart ‚úÖ hard to fix, it requires a cultural change, a shift. Rewarding collaboration and synergies could be an idea.
